Retail strategy · Internship

Smart Point store operations study

This project focuses on understanding how a modern convenience store actually runs each day, where operational friction appears, and how simple process changes can improve the experience for both staff and customers.

Project by Jithendra Mylavarapu · 2025 ↓ Download

Context

Write a short paragraph about the store, your role, and why this project mattered. For example, explain the scale of the store, typical footfall, and which parts of operations you focused on during the internship.

Objectives

  • Map end-to-end store operations from opening to closing.
  • Identify bottlenecks in replenishment and billing.
  • Recommend low-cost, high-impact process improvements.

Approach

Describe what you actually did: time spent on the shop floor, observing peak and non-peak hours, talking to staff, capturing simple data on queues, stockouts, and movement of key SKUs, and how you organized these observations into themes.

Key insights

Summarize a few important patterns you saw, such as where queues formed, how often key items went out of stock on shelves, or how layout affected customer flow and staff effort. Connect each insight to a small action.

Impact & learnings

Explain the recommendations you made, how they were received, and what you learned that is relevant for consulting work: problem-structuring, prioritizing actions, and presenting clear, practical suggestions.
